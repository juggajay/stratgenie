# Complete Summary of 2025 NSW Strata Reforms

The 2025 NSW strata reforms represent the most significant changes to strata law since 2015. These reforms affect how schemes manage finances, support owners in hardship, disclose insurance arrangements, and more. This guide provides a complete overview of what's changed and what you need to do.

## Overview of the Reforms

### What's Changed

The Strata Schemes Management Amendment Act 2024 introduces reforms across several areas:

| Area | Key Changes |
|------|-------------|
| **Financial hardship** | New owner protections and disclosure requirements |
| **Payment plans** | Mandatory consideration of requests |
| **Insurance transparency** | Commission disclosure requirements |
| **Committee accountability** | Enhanced duties and training access |
| **Strata Hub** | Expanded reporting requirements |
| **Sustainability** | Framework for infrastructure improvements |

### When Changes Take Effect

| Change | Effective Date |
|--------|----------------|
| **Financial Hardship Statement** | 1 October 2025 |
| **Payment plan obligations** | 1 October 2025 |
| **Insurance disclosure** | 1 October 2025 |
| **Committee duties (enhanced)** | 1 October 2025 |
| **Sustainability infrastructure** | Various, from 2025 |
| **Strata Hub updates** | Ongoing |

<Info>
Most reforms took effect from 1 October 2025. Some provisions have transitional arrangements or apply only to new situations after that date.
</Info>

## Financial Hardship Requirements

### Financial Hardship Information Statement

From 1 October 2025, every levy notice must include a prescribed statement informing owners about:

- Their right to request a payment plan
- How to make a request
- Where to get further assistance

**What you must do:**
1. Update levy notice templates to include the statement
2. Ensure all notices going forward contain the statement
3. Brief committee on responding to requests

See our [Financial Hardship Statement Guide](/blog/financial-hardship-information-statement-strata) for the full requirements.

### Payment Plan Consideration

When an owner requests a payment plan, the owners corporation must:

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Acknowledge** | Respond to the request promptly |
| **Consider genuinely** | Assess on individual merits |
| **Document** | Record the decision and reasoning |
| **Explain** | Provide reasons if refusing |

**What you must do:**
1. Create a payment plan policy
2. Establish a process for handling requests
3. Train committee on new obligations
4. Keep records of all requests and decisions

See our [Levy Payment Plans Guide](/blog/strata-levy-payment-plans-nsw) for detailed procedures.

## Insurance Transparency

### Disclosure Requirements

Insurance brokers and strata managers must now disclose:

| Disclosure | What Must Be Revealed |
|------------|----------------------|
| **Commissions** | Amount or percentage received |
| **Volume bonuses** | Additional incentives from insurers |
| **Referral fees** | Payments between manager and broker |
| **Related parties** | Ownership connections |
| **Soft benefits** | Non-cash benefits received |

### Timing of Disclosure

Disclosures must be provided:

- Before insurance renewal (for informed decisions)
- At AGM (as part of insurance reporting)
- On request (whenever an owner asks)

**What you must do:**
1. Request disclosure from your broker
2. Request disclosure from your strata manager (if applicable)
3. Include disclosure in AGM insurance reports
4. Keep records of disclosures received

See our [Insurance Commission Disclosure Guide](/blog/disclosure-of-insurance-commissions-strata).

## Committee Accountability

### Enhanced Duties

The reforms reinforce committee member duties:

| Duty | What It Means |
|------|---------------|
| **Care and diligence** | Act reasonably and informed |
| **Good faith** | Prioritise scheme interests |
| **Disclosure** | Declare conflicts of interest |
| **Compliance** | Understand and meet obligations |

### Training Access

NSW Fair Trading now provides:

- Free online training modules
- Specific guidance on the 2025 reforms
- Resources for self-managed schemes
- Regular updates on legislative changes

**What you should do:**
1. Complete Fair Trading training modules
2. Share resources with all committee members
3. Consider training as part of new member induction
4. Document training completed

See our [Committee Training Guide](/blog/strata-committee-mandatory-training-nsw).

## Strata Hub Changes

### Expanded Requirements

The Strata Hub continues to evolve with enhanced requirements for:

| Information | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Scheme details** | Must be kept current |
| **Committee contacts** | Updated after each AGM |
| **Insurance details** | Policy information |
| **Meeting records** | AGM compliance tracking |

### Compliance Tracking

Fair Trading uses Strata Hub data to monitor compliance with:

- AGM holding requirements
- Insurance maintenance
- Registration accuracy

**What you must do:**
1. Ensure Strata Hub registration is current
2. Update after each AGM
3. Keep committee contact details accurate
4. Review periodically for completeness

See our [Strata Hub Registration Guide](/blog/strata-hub-registration-guide).

- Modified voting thresholds for certain works

2. Understand new installation pathways

## Comparison: Before and After

### Financial Hardship

| Aspect | Before | After |
|--------|--------|-------|
| **Levy notices** | No hardship information required | Must include prescribed statement |
| **Payment plan requests** | Could refuse without reason | Must genuinely consider |
| **Documentation** | Optional | Required |
| **Owner awareness** | Varied | Standardised information provided |

### Insurance

| Aspect | Before | After |
|--------|--------|-------|
| **Commission disclosure** | Voluntary | Mandatory |
| **Timing** | On request only | Proactive before renewal |
| **AGM reporting** | Basic details | Must include commissions |
| **Manager disclosure** | Limited | Referral fees must be disclosed |

### Committee

| Aspect | Before | After |
|--------|--------|-------|
| **Training** | No formal resources | Fair Trading modules available |
| **Duties** | General good faith | More explicitly defined |
| **Accountability** | NCAT intervention available | Enhanced intervention powers |
| **Documentation** | Good practice | More strongly expected |

## Penalties and Enforcement

### Non-Compliance Consequences

| Failure | Potential Consequence |
|---------|----------------------|
| **Missing hardship statement** | May affect debt recovery proceedings |
| **Refusing to consider plans** | NCAT may overturn decisions |
| **Insurance non-disclosure** | Regulatory action, licence issues |
| **Strata Hub failures** | Penalties may apply |

### Enforcement Mechanisms

- NSW Fair Trading monitoring and investigation
- NCAT orders and penalties
- Possible personal liability for serious breaches
- Regulatory action against licensed professionals

<Warning>
While enforcement is likely to focus on egregious failures initially, building a culture of compliance now protects your scheme and committee.
</Warning>

## Frequently Asked Questions

### When do the reforms apply?

Most changes took effect 1 October 2025. However, they apply to actions taken after that dateâ€”you don't need to retrospectively update old levy notices, for example.

### Do small schemes need to comply?

Yes. The reforms apply to all NSW strata schemes regardless of size. Some requirements may be simpler for smaller schemes, but they still apply.

### What if we didn't know about the reforms?

Ignorance isn't a defence, but Fair Trading is likely to focus education and enforcement on ensuring future compliance rather than punishing past oversights. Start complying now.

### Are there penalties for non-compliance?

The legislation includes penalty provisions, and non-compliance may affect your ability to pursue debt recovery or defend against NCAT applications. Building good practice protects your scheme.

### Where can we get help implementing the changes?

NSW Fair Trading provides free resources and training. StrataGenie offers tools and guides. For complex situations, consider engaging a strata lawyer or consultant.
