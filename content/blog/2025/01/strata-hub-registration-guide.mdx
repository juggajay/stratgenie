If you're managing a strata scheme in NSW, registering with the Strata Hub is now mandatory. This comprehensive guide walks you through everything you need to know about Strata Hub registration in 2025.

## What is the NSW Strata Hub?

The Strata Hub is an online portal managed by NSW Fair Trading that serves as a central register for all strata schemes in New South Wales. It was introduced as part of the NSW Government's commitment to improving transparency and governance in the strata sector.

<Tip>
All strata schemes in NSW must be registered on the Strata Hub. Failure to register or update your information can result in penalties.
</Tip>

## Who Needs to Register?

Registration is mandatory for:

- All owners corporations in NSW
- Two-lot schemes (including duplexes)
- Large residential schemes
- Mixed-use developments
- Commercial strata schemes

## Information Required for Registration

Before you begin, gather the following information:

1. **Strata Plan Number** - Your SP number (e.g., SP12345)
2. **Scheme Address** - The registered address of your building
3. **Number of Lots** - Total lots in the scheme
4. **Building Details** - Age, type, and key features
5. **Committee Members** - Names and contact details
6. **Insurance Information** - Building insurance policy details
7. **Financial Records** - Current levy amounts

## Step-by-Step Registration Process

### Step 1: Create Your Account

Visit the [NSW Strata Hub](https://www.fairtrading.nsw.gov.au) and create an account using your email address. You'll need to verify your identity as an authorized person for the strata scheme.

### Step 2: Link Your Scheme

Enter your Strata Plan number to link your account to your scheme. If the scheme is already registered, you can request access as a committee member.

### Step 3: Complete Required Information

Fill in all mandatory fields:

- Scheme contact details
- Number of lots and entitlements
- Current strata committee members
- Insurance provider and policy number
- Annual General Meeting date

### Step 4: Upload Documents

Upload the following documents:

- Current insurance certificate
- Latest AGM minutes
- Strata roll

### Step 5: Submit and Verify

Review all information for accuracy before submitting. You'll receive a confirmation email once registration is complete.

<Warning>
Ensure all information is accurate. Incorrect or outdated information may result in compliance issues and potential penalties.
</Warning>

## Common Registration Mistakes

Avoid these common pitfalls:

1. **Outdated Contact Information** - Keep secretary details current
2. **Missing Insurance Updates** - Upload new certificates annually
3. **Incorrect Lot Numbers** - Verify against your strata plan
4. **Forgetting Annual Updates** - Set reminders for annual information review

## Annual Reporting Requirements

Once registered, you must update the Strata Hub annually with:

- AGM dates and outcomes
- Committee changes
- Updated insurance information
- Financial statements
- Any changes to common property

## Penalties for Non-Compliance

The NSW Government takes Strata Hub compliance seriously:

| Offense | Maximum Penalty |
|---------|----------------|
| Failure to register | $2,200 |
| Outdated information | $1,100 |
| False information | $5,500 |

<Danger>
These penalties apply to the owners corporation and can impact all lot owners financially.
</Danger>

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long does registration take?

Initial registration typically takes 15-30 minutes if you have all documents ready.

### Can I update information after registration?

Yes, you can log in and update information at any time. In fact, you're required to keep information current.

### What if our scheme doesn't have a website?

A website is not required. You only need to provide valid contact details for the strata committee.

### Do two-lot schemes need to register?

Yes, all strata schemes in NSW, including two-lot schemes, must register with the Strata Hub.
